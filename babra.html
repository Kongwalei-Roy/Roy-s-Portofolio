A Poem

The Weight of You

Four stanzas, four small truths — for the years behind us, and the ones still ahead.

The Weight of You

I

I have learned the strange arithmetic of you — how someone can take up so little space in a room, and so much in a life. You did not arrive with thunder. You arrived the way light arrives in early spring: unannounced, unhurried, and suddenly everything was easier to bear.

The Weight of You

II

I have tried to name what you do to ordinary time and failed every time — the closest I can come is this: days bend toward you. Even the dull ones. Even the hard ones. They bend, the way a field bends toward whatever sun it's given. People will tell you love is fireworks. I think they've never loved anyone patient enough to become a season instead — seven years of weather, all of it worth standing in.

The Weight of You

III

So here is what I know, plainly, without ornament: you are the kindest accident that ever happened to my life. Not chosen by some grand design — just two people, a room, a beginning, and then everything after that I would choose again, on purpose, every time. Today the world turns one year further around the fact of you. I hope it's gentle. I hope it gives you back even a fraction of what you've given everyone who's ever been lucky enough to know you.

The Weight of You

IV

And if there is any magic left to me, let it be this small, impossible thing — that for one hour today, you could stand outside yourself and see what the rest of us see when we look at you. You would never doubt your worth again.

A Letter, Sealed

For your eyes only

Break the seal to read

Dear Babra,

There are some people who enter our lives so quietly that we don't realize, in that moment, that they are about to become part of our favorite story. Three years ago, I met you through a game. If someone had told me then that a simple conversation would become a friendship, that friendship would become a bond, and that bond would become one of the most meaningful parts of my life, I would never have believed them. Yet here we are. Somewhere between late-night conversations, shared laughter, reading It Ends With Us together, and learning the little details that make you who you are, you became someone incredibly special to me. You became someone I looked forward to talking to. Someone whose messages could brighten an ordinary day. Someone whose presence felt like home, even from miles away.

When I met you for the first time, I remember looking at you and feeling as though reality had somehow become more beautiful than imagination. And when we met again for tea, watching you smile across the table is a memory that time has never managed to steal from me. Then life carried me across an ocean to Boston. But distance has a strange way of revealing what truly matters. No matter how many miles separated us, no matter how much time passed, you remained a constant thought in my heart. There was always a part of me that hoped our paths would cross again. And when I saw you again recently, after all that time, it felt like opening a book I had never truly finished reading. The world around us seemed quieter. For a moment, all I could think was how happy I was that you were standing there in front of me. Today is a special day. It is your birthday. And it is also a day that holds a special place in my heart because it reminds me of us. Out of all the days in the year, it amazes me that the day we celebrate you is also a day that reminds me how grateful I am that you came into my life at all.

Babra, if there is one thing I hope you always know, it is this: you have brought so much joy into my life. More than you realize. You have given me memories I cherish, laughter I still remember, stories I still revisit, and moments that continue to make my heart smile years later. You have a way of making life feel lighter. A way of making ordinary moments feel meaningful. A way of being remembered long after a conversation ends. And for that, I will always be grateful.

I love you.

Not because of grand gestures or perfect moments. I love you because of who you are. Because of your heart. Because of your kindness. Because of the way you show up for the people you care about. Because every chapter of my life that includes you somehow becomes more beautiful.

As you celebrate your birthday, my wish for you is simple: May this year bring you the happiness you deserve. May your dreams find their way to you. May your heart always have reasons to smile. May you be surrounded by love, peace, laughter, and countless blessings. May every door meant for you open. May every prayer whispered in your heart be heard. And may life surprise you with beautiful things when you least expect them.

Thank you for being one of the most wonderful people I have ever known. Thank you for every conversation, every memory, every laugh, and every moment. Most of all, thank you for being you. Happy Birthday, my queen, Babra Jerotich. If I could give you one gift today, it would be the ability to see yourself through my eyes — so you could understand just how special you truly are.

Forever yours, Roy
